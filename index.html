üëõ What It Means for You

Rising egg prices affect food affordability, especially for low-income families. Eggs are a key ingredient in many diets, making price hikes widely felt.

You can:

  • Buy from local farms or co-ops
  • Reduce waste by using all parts of the egg or freezing extras
  • Buy sustainably sourced eggs, and support legislation that enforces more sustainable practices

In the US, you may find labels such as Cage-free, Free-range, or Pasture-raised on your egg cartons. However, the names of these labels can misrepresent what the regulations for having these labels are. It is important to be informed of this as a consumer, as many are misled to believe they are supporting sustainably produced eggs.

Different Labels:

  • Cage-free: This label is regulated by the USDA, but those regulations only require that they are not in a cage, and they can freely roam in an enclosed area with food and water. The regulations don't require that the hens be allowed outside, or specify how big the enclosure must be.
  • Free-range: Another label regulated by the USDA, the regulations only say that the hens must have a way to access the outdoors. The outdoor space could be a relatively small, fenced area, and the times at which the hens can access it is purely up to the farmer's will.
  • Pasture-raised: This label is not regulated by the USDA, so it is important that it is also accompanies by a stamp that says Certified Humane or Animal Welfare Approved. If it does meet these conditions, then it gaurentees that hens have at least 108 square feet of outdoor space, and an equivalent amount of indoor space. If you want to consider supporting sustainable practices, look for this label and accompanying stamp when buying eggs!
